# ANAC — Regulamentacao de Proficiencia Linguistica (Prova ICAO / SDEA)

Research compiled March 2026. Sources: ANAC official portal, RBAC 61, IS 61-003D.

---

## 1. Regulatory Framework

### Primary Regulation
- **RBAC No. 61** — "Licencas, Certificados e Habilitacoes para Pilotos"
- Specifically **Section 61.10** (Proficiencia Linguistica)
- Current version: [RBAC 61 Emenda 14](https://pergamum.anac.gov.br/pergamum/vinculos/RBAC61EMD14.pdf)

### Supplementary Instruction
- **IS 61-003 Revision D** (published September 2, 2024)
- Establishes detailed SDEA exam process, application procedures, and certification process
- [IS 61-003D](https://www.anac.gov.br/assuntos/legislacao/legislacao-1/iac-e-is/is/is-61-003)

### Accreditation Regulation
- **RBAC No. 183** and **IS 183-001 Revision F** — govern accreditation of SDEA entities/examiners
- [IS 183-001F](https://www.anac.gov.br/assuntos/legislacao/legislacao-1/iac-e-is/is/is-183-001f)

### Key RBAC 61, Section 61.10 Provisions
- Pilots of airplanes, helicopters, powered-lift, and airships operating Brazilian aircraft **outside Brazilian airspace** must demonstrate English proficiency via SDEA
- Performance annotated as "English level 4, 5, or 6" OR "English Not Compliant Annex 1" (levels 1-3)
- Only levels 4, 5, 6 may operate internationally
- All Brazilian licenses receive automatic "Portuguese Level 6" annotation

## 2. Who Needs the Exam

| License | SDEA Required? | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| **PPL** (Piloto Privado) | Only for international flights | Not required for domestic-only or to obtain license |
| **CPL** (Piloto Comercial) | Only for international flights | Required before operating internationally |
| **ATPL** (Piloto de Linha Aerea) | Effectively mandatory | ATPL holders almost always operate internationally |
| **Helicopter / Powered-lift / Airship** | Only for international flights | Same rules as fixed-wing |

### Air Traffic Controllers (Separate System)
- ATCs use **EPLIS** (Exame de Proficiencia Linguistica em Ingles do SISCEAB)
- Administered by **ICEA** under **DECEA** (not ANAC)
- Same ICAO scale, same validity rules, but different instrument
- EPLIS has 2 phases: Phase 1 (70% proficiency threshold) → Phase 2 (dual examiner evaluation)

## 3. SDEA — Santos Dumont English Assessment

**The official ANAC language proficiency examination for pilots.**

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Type | Oral interview (face-to-face, NOT written) |
| Context | Routine operations, contingencies, emergencies |
| Scale | ICAO Language Proficiency Rating Scale (6 skills) |
| Scoring | Final level = **lowest score** across 6 skills |
| Examiners | 1 ELE (English Language Expert) + 1 SME (Subject Matter Expert / pilot or ATC) |
| Levels assessed | 1 through 6 (levels 1-3 = "Not Compliant") |
| Stages | Two stages: first for all candidates, second for Level 6 verification only |

## 5. Retake Rules

### The 60-Day Rule
Candidates may only take a new SDEA after **BOTH** conditions are met:
1. **60 calendar days** from previous exam date
2. **Previous result officially disclosed**

### Additional Rules
- No limit on total number of retakes — only the 60-day interval applies
- Level 6 Stage 2 must be taken within **60 days** of Stage 1 (extended from 30 days in IS 61-003D)
- If CAPL requests a technical interview and pilot fails to comply within 60 days → proficiency annotation **revoked**

---

## 6. Validity Periods

| Level | Designation | Validity | Revalidation |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|
| Level 4 | Operational | **3 years** | Must retake SDEA |
| Level 5 | Extended | **6 years** | Must retake SDEA |
| Level 6 | Expert | **Permanent / Lifetime** | No periodic retesting |

**Level 6 caveat:** Level 6 holders are subject to ANAC's **continuous surveillance** program. IS 61-003D (2024) explicitly states even Level 6 holders may be required to retake SDEA if evidence suggests proficiency degradation.

## 7. Recent Changes (2024-2025)

### IS 61-003D (September 2024) — Major Update

1. **Auto Level 4 for US-licensed pilots:** Pilots validating licenses from USA, Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, UK receive **immediate Level 4** without taking SDEA
   - [Full country list](https://www.gov.br/anac/pt-br/assuntos/regulados/profissionais-da-aviacao-civil/processo-de-licencas-e-habilitacoes/proficiencia-linguistica/paises-cuja-licenca-emitida-garante-o-nivel-4-icao)
2. **Level 6 window extended:** 30 → 60 days between Stage 1 and Stage 2
3. **Formal appeals process:** Detailed procedures for candidates to appeal results
4. **Enhanced continuous surveillance:** Explicitly includes Level 6 holders
5. **Continuous accreditation:** Eliminated periodic "edital" system

### 2025 Developments

1. **5 new SDEA examiners credentialed (June 2025):** 1 ELE + 4 SMEs trained at ANAC Rio de Janeiro
2. **Open enrollment for examiners (March 2025):** Applications accepted until March 31, 2025
3. **Procedural adjustments (May 2025):** Due to budget restrictions (Decreto 12,477), candidates must now travel to examiner locations rather than ANAC sending examiners. Exams at credentialed entities continue unchanged.

## 8. Exam Format — First Stage (4 Parts, ~50 minutes)

### Part 1 — Aviation Topics (~5 min)
- General questions about aviation, professional experience, daily pilot activities
- ~1 minute per answer expected
- Purpose: warm-up + general aviation English assessment

### Part 2 — Interacting as a Pilot (~15 min)
- Role-play as pilot "ANAC 123" responding to 5 radiotelephony scenarios
- Each situation: 2 audio recordings, each playable **twice**
- Note-taking permitted
- After each: retell controller communications + interact with examiner
- Purpose: routine and non-routine radiotelephony communication

### Part 3 — Unexpected Situations (~15 min)
- 3 emergency/abnormal situation recordings
- Each played **twice** automatically (5-second pause between plays)
- Report information heard + answer examiner questions
- Discuss difficulty levels and prevention strategies
- Purpose: comprehension and production under stress/emergency

### Part 4 — Picture Description (~10 min)
- Shown aviation-related photograph/figure
- Must: describe image, answer questions, infer causes, evaluate risks, raise hypotheses, sustain arguments, compare with own experience
- Purpose: higher-order language skills (opinion, analysis, hypothesis)

### Evaluation
- Two examiners: ELE + SME
- 6 skills assessed: pronunciation, structure, vocabulary, fluency, comprehension, interactions
- **Final level = lowest rating** across all 6 skills
- Results via email within **10-30 business days**

## 9. Level 6 Two-Stage Process

### Stage 1 — Standard SDEA (all candidates)
- Standard 4-part exam above
- Candidates showing performance **above Level 5 in ALL 6 skills** are identified as Level 6 candidates

### Stage 2 — Level 6 Verification (~45 min)
Must be taken within **60 days** of Stage 1. Five parts:

| Part | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Analyze radiotelephony communications for linguistic/situational breakdowns |
| 2 | Contact ATC and company leadership about abnormal situations |
| 3 | Discuss aviation topics with differing perspectives (debate) |
| 4 | Report aspects of an actual accident recording |
| 5 | Role-play radiotelephony interaction with examiner |

**Key rules:**
- Stage 2 is **optional** — candidates may decline
- If candidate does **not pass** Stage 2 → retains **Level 5** (no downgrade risk)
- Results disclosed by ANAC within **20 days**
- Level 6 = permanent validity (subject to surveillance)

### Historical Context
- ANAC **suspended** Level 6 issuance for a period
- **Resumed March 2020**, then again **December 2022** with current two-stage system
- 2022 resumption brought Brazil into alignment with full ICAO 1-6 range

## 10. Domestic vs International Requirements

| Operation | English Required? |
|-----------|------------------|
| Domestic flights within Brazilian airspace | **No** |
| International flights departing Brazil | **Yes** (minimum Level 4) |
| Flights outside Brazilian airspace | **Yes** (minimum Level 4) |

### Practical Implications
- PPL/CPL holder flying domestic-only = **no SDEA needed**
- English proficiency is **not** a prerequisite for obtaining any Brazilian pilot license
- Pilots without valid annotation or with "English Not Compliant Annex 1" → can operate freely within Brazilian airspace
- ATPL holders: effectively mandatory because airlines operate internationally
- All Brazilian licenses get automatic "Portugues Nivel 6" (not tested)
