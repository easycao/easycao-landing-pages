# ICAO Doc 9835 — Manual on the Implementation of Language Proficiency Requirements

Reference document for content creation. Source: ICAO Doc 9835 AN/453 (2nd Edition, 2010).

---

## Overview

Doc 9835 is the official ICAO manual guiding implementation of language proficiency requirements for pilots and air traffic controllers. The requirements originate from amendments to ICAO Annexes 1, 6, 10, 11, and PANS-ATM (adopted March 2003).

**Key facts:**
- Assesses **speaking and listening only** (no reading/writing)
- Context: aeronautical radiotelephony communication
- Level 4 (Operational) = minimum for international flights
- Overall level = **lowest score** across 6 criteria

---

## The 5 Holistic Descriptors

These define what it means to be a proficient speaker (Annex 1, Appendix 1, §4.5.3):

1. Communicate effectively in voice-only AND face-to-face situations
2. Communicate on common, concrete and work-related topics with accuracy and clarity
3. Use appropriate communicative strategies to exchange messages and resolve misunderstandings
4. Handle successfully the linguistic challenges of complications or unexpected turns of events
5. Use a dialect or accent intelligible to the aeronautical community

## Complete ICAO Language Proficiency Rating Scale

### Level 1 — Pre-Elementary
All criteria: Performs below Elementary level.

### Level 2 — Elementary

| Criterion | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | Heavily influenced by L1, usually interferes with understanding |
| Structure | Limited control of few simple memorized patterns |
| Vocabulary | Isolated words and memorized phrases only |
| Fluency | Very short isolated memorized utterances, frequent pausing, distracting fillers |
| Comprehension | Limited to isolated memorized phrases, carefully and slowly articulated |
| Interaction | Slow response, often inappropriate, limited to simple routine exchanges |

### Level 3 — Pre-Operational

| Criterion | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | Influenced by L1, **frequently** interferes with understanding |
| Structure | Basic patterns not always well controlled, errors **frequently** interfere with meaning |
| Vocabulary | Often sufficient for common topics but limited range, word choice often inappropriate, often unable to paraphrase |
| Fluency | Produces stretches but phrasing/pausing often inappropriate, hesitations may prevent effective communication |
| Comprehension | Often accurate on common topics, **may fail** with complications or unexpected events |
| Interaction | Sometimes immediate, can maintain exchanges on familiar topics, **generally inadequate** with unexpected events |

### Level 4 — Operational (MINIMUM REQUIRED)

| Criterion | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | Influenced by L1, **only sometimes** interferes with understanding |
| Structure | Used creatively, **usually well controlled**, errors rarely interfere with meaning |
| Vocabulary | **Usually sufficient**, can **often** paraphrase when lacking vocabulary |
| Fluency | Appropriate tempo, occasional loss of fluency on transition from rehearsed to spontaneous speech, fillers not distracting |
| Comprehension | **Mostly accurate**, may be slower or require clarification with complications |
| Interaction | **Usually immediate**, initiates and maintains exchanges, deals adequately with misunderstandings |

### Level 5 — Extended

| Criterion | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | **Rarely** interferes with understanding |
| Structure | Basic consistently well controlled, complex attempted but with errors that sometimes interfere |
| Vocabulary | Sufficient for effective communication, paraphrases **consistently and successfully**, sometimes idiomatic |
| Fluency | Speaks at length with relative ease, uses appropriate discourse markers |
| Comprehension | Accurate on common topics, **mostly accurate** with complications, comprehends range of speech varieties |
| Interaction | **Immediate**, manages speaker/listener relationship effectively |

### Level 6 — Expert

| Criterion | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pronunciation | **Almost never** interferes with understanding |
| Structure | Both basic and complex **consistently well controlled** |
| Vocabulary | Wide variety of topics, **idiomatic, nuanced, sensitive to register** |
| Fluency | Natural effortless flow, varies speech stylistically, uses discourse markers spontaneously |
| Comprehension | **Consistently accurate** in nearly all contexts, includes linguistic and cultural subtleties |
| Interaction | Interacts with ease in nearly all situations, sensitive to verbal and non-verbal cues |

## Key Progression Keywords (for content creation)

| Criterion | L3 → L4 shift | L4 → L5 shift |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Pronunciation | "frequently" → "only sometimes" | "only sometimes" → "rarely" |
| Structure | "not always well controlled" → "usually well controlled" | "usually" → "consistently" |
| Vocabulary | "often unable to paraphrase" → "can often paraphrase" | "often" → "consistently" |
| Fluency | "may prevent communication" → "does not prevent" | "occasional loss" → "speaks at length with ease" |
| Comprehension | "may fail" → "mostly accurate" | "mostly" → "accurate" |
| Interaction | "generally inadequate" → "usually immediate" | "usually" → "immediate" |

## Validity Periods

| Level | Validity | Retesting |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Level 4 | 3 years | Must retest |
| Level 5 | 6 years | Must retest |
| Level 6 | Lifetime | No retesting (some States set 9 years) |

## Testing Methodology

### Delivery Methods
- **Direct (face-to-face):** Live interaction — most reliable
- **Semi-direct (computer-assisted):** Computer prompts, human raters
- **Indirect (fully computerized):** Not recommended for licensing without human intervention

### Rater Requirements
- Must be familiar with Doc 9835
- Initial and annual training for standardization
- Tests must assess listening separately from speaking

### Test Design Principles
- Must satisfy holistic criteria
- Elicit language in aviation-relevant contexts
- Assess both routine and non-routine/emergency situations
- Tests both standardized phraseology and plain language

---

## Plain Language vs Phraseology

ICAO recognizes language endangers safety through:
1. Incorrect/non-existent use of standardized phraseologies
2. Deficiencies in plain language proficiency
3. Combined use of several languages in same airspace

Pilots must be proficient in plain language because phraseology cannot cover every situation — especially non-routine and emergencies.
